Eclipse


Formal work on textile tension upstream of the project. Choice of the same system for all experiments. Each is composed of a cardboard cylinder covered with a fabric (here polyester jersey). The fabric is then pulled down to create a hollow.

Based on previous research and the theme of the refuge, I chose to design an object to protect myself from the sun. For this I reversed the direction of my experiments, the fabric is now pulled upwards to design a parasol. Thus, the typical shape of parasols is created by the textile tension. It also helps to purify the shape by avoiding the use of many articulated metal arms.

This parasol is composed of an aluminum pole on which is hung a pole of 1.60m in diameter covered with a cotton and polyester fabric. The hoop is lifted by a rope hanging from the center of the fabric (to make a cone when stretched) and passing through the top of the mast, which is 2m50. The rope is finally retained by a cleat on the mast.
